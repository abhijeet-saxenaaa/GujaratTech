#237283

Process of spontaneous dewatering of feed solution using salt bitterns as draw solutions

Akhand Pratap Singh, Ankit Kumar, Ankit Kumar, Ankit Kumar, Ankit

Spontaneous dewatering of a feed solution using salt bitterns as draw solutions is a process where water is naturally removed from the feed solution through osmosis. Salt bitterns, which are concentrated brine solutions left after salt extraction, have a high osmotic pressure. When these bitterns are used as a draw solution, they pull water out of the feed solution across a semi-permeable membrane. This process, known as forward osmosis, concentrates the feed solution and dilutes the salt bitterns, enabling efficient dewatering without applying external energy.

#235272

Eco-friendly process for the isolation of biopolymers from agricultural residues

Siddharth, Shivkumar

The eco-friendly process for isolating biopolymers from agricultural residues begins with collecting agricultural waste, such as rice husks or wheat straw. The residues are then cleaned to remove impurities through washing and drying. To extract the biopolymers, environmentally safe methods are used, often involving the use of water or mild chemicals, instead of harsh solvents. The residues may undergo processes like enzymatic treatment or mechanical grinding to break down the material and release biopolymers like cellulose, hemicellulose, or lignin. After extraction, the biopolymers are purified and dried, making them suitable for use in various applications, such as biodegradable plastics or sustainable packaging. The entire process minimizes the use of harmful chemicals, making it eco-friendly and sustainable.

#342523

Smart Soil Moisture Sensor with Integrated Water Management System

David Mrtin, Felder kevin, Schmitt Rainer

This patent describes an innovative soil moisture sensor that not only measures soil moisture levels but also integrates with a water management system for efficient irrigation. The sensor uses advanced materials and IoT technology to provide real-time soil moisture data. It can communicate wirelessly with a central control system, which analyzes the data and automatically adjusts irrigation schedules. The system also considers weather forecasts and plant-specific water requirements to optimize water usage. This technology significantly reduces water waste in agriculture and landscaping while promoting healthier plant growth.
